A. Demand (Load Data)
* Hourly electricity demand (MW), ideally 1 year (or start with 1 week)
* Potential sources: PJM Interconnection, CAISO, ERCOT, U.S. Energy Information Administration

B. Generator Fleet Data
Each generator should have:
* Capacity (MW) -	Max output
* Min generation - Technical minimum
* Heat rate -	Efficiency
* Fuel type	- Gas, coal, nuclear, wind
* Variable cost -	$/MWh
* Startup cost - (for UC)
* Ramp rate -	MW/hour

Source: EIA Form 860
Tip: Start with a simplified synthetic fleet (5–10 generators), then scale up.

C. Renewable Generation Profiles (Time-varying output)
* Sources: NREL (best!), Wind & solar capacity factors (hourly)
* What you need: Solar CF (0–1), Wind CF (0–1)

D. Fuel Price
* Used to compute generation costs.
* Sources: U.S. Energy Information Administration

E. Optional Advanced Data
* Transmission limits (for DC OPF)
* Emissions rates (for carbon pricing scenarios)
